Preface & Contents

This page provides background reading and general discussions on Neural Radiance Fields (NeRF) and Gaussian Splatting (GS). In "Paper Reviews", I examine papers on 3-D, 3-D+Mod, 4-D and 4-D+Mod*. In "Lectures on..." I explain general features/characteristics/etc. of GS and NeRF that I found useful for understanding 3-D reconstruction research. Overall, the aim of this is to decypher (often) cryptic papers to allow myself and others to learn something useful.

As a bit of background, I am completing my PhD in Intelligent Cinmeatography (yr 3). This tackles inverse graphics challenges using GS and NeRF reconstruction in the context of film-making (dubbed 'cinematography'). My research has focuses on accessibile solutions, i.e. low VRAM, small model sizes, sparse-view datasets. My research also focuses on dynamic scenes, whereby representations are expected to be implicity dynamic, or deformable. Compared with static reconstruction a whole new array of materials (edge cases) are introduced. For example, dynamic textures (fire, smoke, etc.), dynamic reflective objects, dynamic transparent objects and non-rigid motions (hair, stetching, etc.). Anyways, this is all to say that in most disscussion sections, I will be evaluating work based how the work fits into dynamic/4-D reconstruction pipelines. This includes thinking about how static/3-D research fits into 4-D.

* "Mod" indicates papers related to 3-D/4-D NeRF/GS but do not tackle challenges that are core to NeRF and GS. E.g. point segmentation or GS re-lighting
