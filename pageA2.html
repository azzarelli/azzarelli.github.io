Smart Meter

Project Description:

[This is a University project which is equivalent to one module]
  Aim: to build a commercial smart meter that would be used within a moving Medical site which would opperate in areas of large struggle.
Method: Each team is allocated six EEE students and has the goal of building a powersupply, housing, interface cricuitry and software that makes up the smart meter. You are given 25£ budget with 7-week deadline and are expected to log individual progress in logbooks to show your involvement within the project. Each subteam has a set of standards that the team must abide by to be successfully marked.

Team Structure:

I was the Team Leader. Other roles included: Treasurer (for 25£ spending limit) and Team Organiser (for meetings date/time, minutes etc.)
As the Lead I divided the team into Power supply, Housing, Interface and Programming Subteams. I took the role as Head of Programming where I developed the control system, LCD interface and additional (wireless) interfaces. Additionally I helped with Housing team with the initial design and the interface circuitry with building simulation models on Multisim

My Involvement:

[Programming, C]
  I implemented a basic multi-path control system. The system develops an out-come (not always 'output') depending on the current state of the Medical centre (described by inputs). Its relatively simple in design however serveral minimisation techniques were used to ensure that the concept acts more efficiently. This is important in Medical situations where everything is time-pressured. I also implemented an LCD interface which displays key results with an additional graph-features to create a better looking UI.
[Programming, C++]
  As an additional feature I built a wireless access point (WAP) using a NodeMCU (Arduino with WIFI module) which hosted a website (using local WIFI) which displayed the same values as the LCD interface with additional calculations. The site is smartphone-friendly and incorporates controlability using HTTP POST and GET input methods to control the LCD interface as well as manually changing the current state of the Medical center.
[Housing]
  I developed the intial housing model. I implemented "intsert" walls that would prevent water from entering the body. As the 3D printers provided were relatively small I designed the levels of the body to be stacked and screwed from the inside to create a strong structure. Features within the hosuing consist of ledges for rest the LCD screen in and particular positioning of screws for PCB and micrcontroller mounting.
[Interface Circuitry]
  I built and modeled a PWM output signal which was used to control an boost the output of signals from 3.3V (microcontroller output) to 5V (required output).

Review Process and Outcomes

[First Review]
  I develpoed a six-person presentation to convey the progress of each sub-team in the first two weeks of this project. We scored 8/10, which is a testament to our rehersals and planning that was made. We were also assessed on the Housing design where we scored 5/5.
[Second Review]
  Our individual log books (Progress books) were marked for which I recieved a 7/10. I do believe this reflected the work I had, and I knew that I would need to increase in quality for a higher mark. As a group we were graded on our power supply design (scored 5/5) and the 3D housing model (scored 5/5).
[Final Review]
  In the final (seventh) week the group was assessed on our individual contribution towards the project. This mark was the largest given mark contributing to 25% of the grade. For this I ensured that my section of the project was completely developed with additional features and because of the +150 page report I scored full mark (25/25). I am proud of this as assessment writing is something I had struggled with on the path, however have only recently overcome it.
Regarding the final group report, I co-wrote it with another team memeber. Within the report I described our final product as well as the team's achievements of which I was proud of (as a team leader). I also answered "what if..." questions, detailing what would have gone on if we were given an industry sized budget with an industry sized time-scheme. For this report we scored 11/15.
Finally we were given marks for the final product which had, rather dissapointingly, ran through issues during the final test, scoring 13/20. Though our product worked well we a lost majority of marks building a machine that did not comply much with the project specification. The reasoning behind this decision was made by me. I wanted to create a smart-meter with aditional functionalitites as when faced with competition in a working environment products need to have unique selling points and in following with this aspect of the project takss, I wanted to create a unique device.

Project Summary:

  In my university career this is the largest project I have been able to take part of (worth 1 Module, 7 Weeks). Knowing this I needed to make sure I had full control of my own mark and to do this I elected myself as the Team Leader. My final mark (79/100) was one of the highest scoring. My primary role as 'Leader' was to ensure that my team had the correct tools and understnading to complete the given tasks to the highest level. I used kaban-style list-making applications (Trello) as well as strong messaging-document transfer applications (Slack) to ensure nothing was mis-communicated and within my programming team I set up a GitHub with multi-branch implementation and taught my team memebers to use it effectively. Personally, devdeloping my embedded C/C++ skills fruther allowed me to develop a stronger understanding of connected devices (using WAP, WIFI-module etc.), and implementing these skill within a simulated work environment pushed me to understand the difficulties behind designing electronic products.
So far I do believe (particularly) this project is a testament of my work as a Team Leader and a programmer.

Competition Title: Blog-style post about your ideas of
             "The Future of Cyber-Security for Connected and Autonomous Vehicles"
  When I began drafting the post, I was attending a conference about the importance of the regulatory sector to SMEs within the Aerospace industry. This influence expresses the first theme within my essay: 'Regulations within Cyber-Security'. In my eyes this is an imperative point to make. Regulations dictate company growth in any economic market (to an extent); within the securities industry it plays an even larger role, as 'messing up' impacts a lot more people than other industries. Take for example the Adobe hack in 2013 where 150 million accounts had been hacked - that's a crazy-large amount. Therefore within my essay I felt compelled to express the opinion that regulations is always going to be a main focus for the cyber-security industry.
  Another prevelant theme you may notice is that of Hollywood's role within the industry. I know - I know - this sounds far-fetched, but it's not. The influence that 'the movies' have on a young mind is great; it's the reason I took Maths more seriously at High-school after watching 'Good Will Hunting' and good-Will's brain at work. And so the effect that movies like James Bond and [insert generic spy movie] have on young kids is also pretty significant. Obviously a majority of these kids will be deterred sooner-or-later by the difficulty of making it in the securities industry (perhaps the passion for acting has overcome them), but those that stick it out will most likely tell you that spy movies did play an influential role. Therefore I felt that the theme of Hollywood was appropriate for such an essay.
  The last theme I stuck to describes the unicity of successful tech industries, such as the phone-software industry which has two major players, IOS and Android. This industry governance is something that is missing within vehicle softwares, though to be fair it's still in the infancy stage of development. It is something I hope to see play more of an active role in car-selling and with the slow rise of electric vehicles, the software that grows with it is becoming evermore paramount. The issues I raise with the multiplicity of car-softwares is that of errors. Errors that are bad. Very bad in cars. It is one of the reasons you will find minimal errors in developed, unified industry products such as phones. And it is also why it is more difficult to provide system protection for products that compete with a lot of players, like cars. Smaller competition means larger allocation of resources and therefore by logic should make your system more infallible, and curently there are too many players in the game.
  So you see, this is how I came to the conclusion that the future of cyber-security for connected and autonomous vehicles depends on a unified industry, that thrives alongside a strong regulatory system with the aspiration of making a machine so secure a James Bond villain couldn't hack it.

Public Speaking and Organisation

Speaking Your Mind - Influences

Using influence when writing a speech is as much a technical tool as a pencil to painters tool (in the way that you use a pencil to plan and not to paint). You should not be looking to mimic your influencers, you should only be using them to structure your speech. This took me a long time to learn. Additionally, inluencers are not a constant when writing your speech. If I use Einstein to speak about Physics great! However, if I keep using Einstein to speak about Physics I will run out of Einstein-related Physics quickly. Varying your influences is important when speaking as it shows you have made effort to research. Given this information I would like to say it doesn't matter where you draw your influence.
  During high-school, my English teachers would draw inspiration from speeches that took place at UN conferences (I remeber analysing Emma Watson and Malala Yousafzai in class). This type of influence always confused me because my teachers would idealise the sum of techniques used, where the only consistent technique would be that of pragmatic clarity (the need for this being: translating a quick, complex speech is more difficult than a clear, concise speech). Envisioning myself in such a position as Ms Watson infront of the UN allows me to draw clarity into speech writing.
  In the situations when I want to find technical influence I fall towards well-structure pannel discussions. When speaking I don't want to come off 'robotic' by ticking off a list of technniques as i speak. What I want is to come off as someone who passionately understands what they are saying and can communicate it easily. This is where pannel discussions can influence the "ease-of-speaking" aspect.
  I am not going to tell you where to find your sources or who to look at for influence because frankly it does not matter. It really doesn't. As I said before influence is like a pencil to a paintbrush - in this case not all painters need to use a pencil to plan.
